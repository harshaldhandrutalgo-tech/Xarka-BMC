The Stakes for BMC

The Brihanmumbai Municipal Corporation is one of the world's largest urban local bodies — administering public health, water supply, roads, solid waste management, education, disaster response, and civic services for over 1.26 crore residents. The volume of data generated — citizen records, medical files, infrastructure surveys, tax assessments, enforcement logs — is staggering and deeply sensitive.

As BMC digitises its operations and explores AI-assisted governance, one question becomes non-negotiable: where does this data go, and who controls it?

Most commercial AI tools in use today — whether chatbots, document summarisers, or translation services — are powered by servers located in the United States or China. Every query sent to these tools carries the risk of transmitting sensitive municipal data across international borders, outside the jurisdiction of Indian law.

Where Does Your Data Go Today?

When a BMC officer uses a mainstream AI assistant to summarise a report, translate a document, or draft a notice, that text — along with any data embedded in it — is transmitted over the internet to servers owned by American or Chinese technology corporations. This is not a theoretical risk. It is how these services are architected.

Xarka's Indigenous Indian LLM

Beyond the question of where data goes lies an equally important question: which model processes it? Western AI models are trained primarily on English-language data from Western contexts. They underperform on Indian languages, misread Indian administrative terminology, and lack the contextual knowledge required for Mumbai's diverse, multilingual, multi-script civic environment.

Xarka's answer is not to adapt a foreign model. It is to have built one from scratch — in India, for India.

Why Xarka for BMC

The question for BMC is not whether to adopt AI — the operational efficiency gains are undeniable, and peer municipalities globally are already realising them. The question is how to adopt AI in a way that does not create new risks for the citizens BMC is constitutionally obligated to serve.

Foreign AI providers offer privacy assurances through terms of service. Xarka offers privacy through architecture. Because the model runs inside BMC's infrastructure, it is physically incapable of transmitting data to any external party — regardless of what legal changes occur in the US or China, regardless of data breach incidents at foreign companies, and regardless of geopolitical developments that might put Indian government data at risk.

Mumbai is not just India's financial capital — it is home to the dreams and livelihoods of over a crore people. The AI systems that serve them must be as trustworthy, as local, and as accountable as the institution they support.

Choosing indigenous Indian AI is also an act of national self-determination. Every rupee invested in Indian LLMs builds capability that stays in India, creates technology jobs in India, and ensures that the AI layer governing public services reflects Indian values, Indian languages, and Indian law — not Silicon Valley priorities or Beijing's strategic interests.
